# Cover Letter — SWE Role (Sample)

I first encountered the kind of problem your team is working on during my time
at LumaDent, where I built tooling for radiologists who were making
high-stakes decisions under significant time pressure. The work taught me that
good software is not primarily about technical sophistication — it is about
identifying the exact moment where a person's focus breaks and doing everything
in your power to protect it. That principle has guided everything I have built
since.

At SlatePath.ai I had the unusual experience of being both the person writing
the code and the person deciding what code was worth writing. That dual
vantage point sharpened my instincts considerably. I learned to move quickly
without confusing speed with haste, and to make architectural decisions that
could accommodate the things I did not yet know I would need. The multi-tenant
LLM platform we shipped went from a sketch on a whiteboard to ten thousand
active users in eight months, and the decisions I made in week two were still
holding up in month eight — not because I predicted the future, but because I
built things that were easy to change.

What draws me to your team specifically is the combination of technical depth
and genuine product ambition. I do my best work in environments where
engineering and product thinking are treated as inseparable, where the question
"can we build this?" is always asked alongside "should we, and for whom?" I
would welcome the chance to bring that perspective to the problems you are
working on.
